# Product Vision

## The Problem

Getting a good teacher is luck.

Not all teachers take teaching seriously. For many, it is a fallback, not a calling. The quality of a child's foundational education is therefore not a function of the child's ability or potential. It is a function of which classroom they happened to walk into.

The system compounds this. Academic performance is measured on tests that are largely unidimensional. A child who understands the material but does not process it the way the test expects will score poorly. Score poorly enough times, and the label sticks. Smart or stupid. That label shapes what the child attempts, what they avoid, and what they believe they are capable of, often for the rest of their life.

Most children are not aware that there are different ways to learn. They are not capable of that level of introspection at that age. To them, failure means they are not smart enough. It rarely occurs to them, or their parents, that the material was simply never presented in a way that matched how they think.

I know this personally. I was good at mathematics until problems grew complex enough that they required holding several variables in parallel. I avoided those problems entirely. Not because I could not solve them, but because the first few steps were not immediately visible to me. I did not know this about myself. A teacher noticed it, named it, and showed me a different way to enter the problem. My performance changed immediately.

That teacher could just as easily have never noticed. Or not cared enough to say anything.


## What We Are Building

A tutoring system that understands how a specific child learns, then teaches accordingly.

Not a generic adaptive system that adjusts difficulty based on scores. The product builds a longitudinal model of how a child thinks: what kinds of problems they avoid, where they consistently make errors, whether they need the big picture before the procedure, whether they shut down when they feel judged, whether they benefit from being nudged toward the answer or from being shown the method directly.

This model is built through observation, not self-reporting. A child cannot accurately describe their own behavioral pattern. A parent cannot either. The system watches how the child behaves during problem-solving across sessions and builds the model from that behavioral signal over time.

Session one is a calibration session. The system presents the same concept through different approaches and observes which produces engagement, which produces avoidance, and which produces the right kind of struggle. The child is learning. The system is watching how.

Every subsequent session refines the model. The longer a child uses the product, the more precisely it understands them.

The parent receives periodic insight reports. Not just progress scores. Observations. The kind of thing a good teacher would tell a parent at a conference: here is a pattern I have noticed in how your child works, and here is what we are doing about it.


## Who This Is For

The immediate customer is the immigrant South Asian parent in North America and Europe who already knows their child is being underchallenged by the local curriculum. This parent is already spending money on remote tutors from India to close that gap. The tutors are good, but the model has failure points: scheduling dependency, inconsistent quality, tutors who move on.

This product replaces that tutor. Same outcome. Available whenever the child wants to work. Gets better the more the child uses it, unlike a human tutor whose model of the child is limited by session frequency and memory.

The child is the user. The parent is the buyer.


## The Founding Belief

Every child deserves access to someone who understands them better than they understand themselves.

The difference between a child who believes they are capable and one who has already decided they are not often comes down to a single adult who looked closely enough to see how that child actually thinks, and cared enough to say something.

We are building that at scale.


## What This Is Not

This is not a content library. Khan Academy has more content than we will ever need to build.

This is not a generic AI assistant with memory. The memory is not a chat log. It is a structured model of a specific child's cognitive patterns, built from behavioral observation and refined continuously.

This is not a test prep tool. Test performance is a byproduct. The goal is that a child stops avoiding problems they do not immediately know how to start.
